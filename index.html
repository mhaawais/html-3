Biryani Recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Basmati rice (soaked for 30 minutes)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 2 green cardamoms
  • 1 black cardamom
  • 1 small cinnamon stick
  • 500g chicken/mutton
  • 2 onions (thinly sliced)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • ½ cup yogurt
  • 2 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • ½ tsp cumin powder
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil/ghee
  • Salt to taste
  • A handful of fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
  • A handful of mint leaves (chopped)
  • A few strands of saffron soaked in ¼ cup warm milk
  • 1 tbsp fried onions (for extra flavor)

Method

  1. Boil water in a pot, add bay leaves, cloves, cardamoms, cinnamon, and salt.
  2. Add soaked rice and cook until 70% done (the grains should still be firm).
  3. Drain the water and set the rice aside.
  4. Heat oil/ghee in a pan, add sliced onions, and sauté until golden brown.
  5. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for 1 minute until fragrant.
  6. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  7. Add chicken/mutton, mix well, and cook until meat changes color.
  8. Add red chili powder, turmeric, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt.
  9. Add yogurt, mix well, cover, and cook for 15-20 minutes (until the meat is tender).
  10. Sprinkle garam masala and add chopped mint & coriander leaves.
  11. In a large pot, add a layer of cooked rice, then a layer of prepared chicken/mutton masala.
  12. Repeat layering, ending with rice on top.
  13. Drizzle saffron milk over the top layer and add fried onions.
  14. Cover the pot with a tight lid and cook on low heat for 15-20 minutes.
  15. Turn off the heat and let it rest for 10 minutes before opening.
  16. Fluff the biryani gently with a fork.
  17. Serve hot with raita (yogurt sauce) & salad.
